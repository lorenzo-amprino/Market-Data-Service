# Use Instrument and Listing price series as ingestion consistency boundaries

Market Data Service will not provide globally atomic updates across the entire Universe during ingestion. Instead, it will use two main consistency boundaries: a Financial Instrument aggregate for canonical identity, lifecycle, metadata, dividends, corporate actions, listing relationships, and Preferred Listing selection; and a Listing price series aggregate for daily Observed Prices of a single Listing. This keeps high-volume price ingestion independent from instrument reference-data updates, avoids locking or rewriting an entire instrument aggregate for routine price refreshes, and means APIs may expose mixed freshness across different instruments or listings; consumers must rely on Data Availability, Data Provenance, and retrieval timestamps to assess each returned fact.
